Aesthetic elements for a fine prose: talent, affection and humour

Guest speaker: Dr. Choi Yick Wai 
Date: 17 November 2017
Time: 2:30pm-4:00pm
Venue: University Bookstore

Introduction
Prose is free in form. It resides next to one’s soul and mind. It is composed by the finest elements of talent, affection and humour that it is able to deliver an inspiring and refreshing aesthetic experience. Focusing on writing elements such as wisdom, talent and humour, this talk will share the secret of creating a moving atmosphere for prose through the sharing of speaker’s experience and the appreciation of classic works.

CAC Activities Review: Writing within 100 words: Workshop of Diagram-and-text Writing

Instructor : Mr. Ho Ka Chun
Date : 6 November 2017
Time : 4:00-5:30pm
Venue : ELB_202

Introduction
Diagram-and-text writing is becoming more popular in the print media and the Internet. Excellent diagram-and-text works can catch people’s eyes and convey messages effectively. This kind of writing is usually short that someone may underrate it, but in fact, it is challenging that requires certain skills to make the work impressive and inspiring. In this workshop, we will learn some skills to read a picture and write for it through group activities and individual writing practice. All in all, we will focus on:
• The multiple relationships of diagram and text;
• The skills of reading a picture ;
• Learning from experienced diagram-and-text writers' works;
• Individual writing practice under guidance.

CAC Activities Review: Rolling Fun, Rolling Transliteration: Introduction to Jyutping

Instructor: Dr. Cheng Siu Pong
Date：2 ,9 November 2017
Time：2:30 -4:00 pm, 10：30-12:00am
Venue：ICS LT1, YIA LT3

Introduction
Jyutping, the Linguistic Society of Hong Kong Cantonese Romanisation Scheme, is an important tool for transcribing Cantonese. User can easily transcribe Cantonese by remembering the Pinyin characters and the simple rules of the system. In addition, users can adopt Jyutping for transliteration and for pronunciation of unusual words. This workshop will introduce Jyutping and help students to master the Romanisation system in a short time.

CAC Activities Review: Standard Accent: Cantonese Pronunciation Workshop

Instructor: Dr. Law Yin Ling
Date: 26 October , 7 November 2017
Time: 2:30-4:00 pm, 3：30-5：00pm
Venue: ICS LT1, HTB_B6

Introduction
Cantonese is the daily /conversational language of Hong Kong, but its theoretical knowledge is not popular. The situation of “knowing without understanding” or “completely not knowing” causes questions of lazy syllables and mispronunciation. This workshop will introduce the basic concepts of Initial, Vowel, and Tone, as well as analysing and solving the common problems that Hong Kong people encounter in pronouncing Cantonese.

CAC Activities Review: National Putonghua Proficiency Test (PSC) Preparation and Practice Workshop

Instructor:　Ms. Wong On Ki
Date : 24 October and 14, 21 November 2017
Time : 2:30-4:00pm
Venue: LSK_416, LSK_416, LSK_408L

Introduction 
The National Putonghua Proficiency Test (PSC) is the language test system developed by the Institute of Applied Linguistics. Students with appropriate qualifications for the PSC may apply for exemption from the Language Proficiency Assessment for Teachers (LPAT). And it is designed to test the language ability for students who need to use Putonghua in their further studies or workplaces. Our focus will be on:
-Introducing the content of the Test.
-Developing students’ skills in each section of the exam.
-Analysing errors of pronunciation though the case studies.
-Providing an authentic exam experience with feedbacks and comments.
-Helping students to plan a better study approach. 
-Offering 20 minutes one-to-one personalised feedback and help from our tutors on how to improve students’ performance in the exam.

Chinese Classic Texts and Technology Workshop
Date：20 September 2017
Time：2:30 pm-4 pm
Venue：Pi Chiu R103+105
Instructor: Dr. LAW Yin Ling

Introduction：The digitalisation of ancient books provides not only a new reading style but also a convenient research platform to beginners. The CUHK Library has a large electronic collection of ancient text that includes several databases, such as the CHANT Database, the Scripta Sinica, and the Chinese Rare Book Digital Collection. These databases provide online access to ancient text copies ranging from literature, history, philosophy, arts, culture, religion, science and business. Utilising the functions of the database will be a stepping stone for students to enhance the result of studying with the least effort.
