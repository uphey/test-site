CAC Workshops Review: Mandarin self-assessment and self-studying Workshop (12 sessions)

Speaker: Miss Wong On Ki
Date: From 23 Jan 2018 to 16 April 2018 (1 session per week)
Time: 10:30am to 12:00pm, 2:30pm to 4:00pm, 3:30pm to 5:00pm
Venue: LSK_416L, LSK_410L, LSK_401L
Language: Mandarin

Introduction: The National Putonghua Proficiency Test (PSC) is the language test system developed by the Institute of Applied Linguistics. Students with appropriate qualifications for the PSC may apply for exemption from the Language Proficiency Assessment for Teachers (LPAT). And it is designed to test the language ability for students who need to use Putonghua in their further studies or workplaces. Our focus will be on:

  • Introducing the content of the Test.
  • Developing students’ skills in each section of the exam.
  • Analysing errors of pronunciation though the case studies.
  • Providing an authentic exam experience with feedbacks and comments.
  • Helping students to plan a better study approach.
  • Offering 20 minutes one-to-one personalised feedback and help from our tutors on how to improve students’ performance in the exam.

CAC Workshops Review: Workplace language: Self-recommendation letter and self-introduction

Speaker: Dr. Ng Ka Yi
Date: 15 March 2018
Time: 3:30pm to 5:00pm
Venue: ELB_403
Language: Cantonese

Introduction: When applying exchange, internship programme or occupational post, students will need to prepare for oral or written self-recommendation. How will students from the school of Journalism and Communication comprehend the artful rhetoric of this aspect? Is “love to interact with people, good at communication” a quality for a public relation job? Can “having awareness to contemporary issues” be the justification of applying as a journalist? Time: 3:30pm to 5:00pm

Language: Cantonese

CAC Workshops Review: Grinding ink and flourishing brushes- appreciating and writing spring couplet

Speaker︰Dr. Wong Siu Yung
Date: 1 February 2018 (Cantonese) /8 February 2018 (Mandarin)
Time: 10:30am to 12 pm/ 2:30pm to 4 pm
Venue: WMW_408 / ELB_308
Language: Cantonese/ Mandarin

Introduction: Couplet, the traditional art of China, has numerous functions and types. Among all the varieties, spring couplet is the most common one. During the Lunar New Year, every family will put up spring couplets that express their wishes of getting out of the old and in with the new, determination towards designated objectives and aspiration for the future. Spring couplet carries not only practical functions, but also its artistic values can display the beauty of words. Therefore, it is necessary for students to learn about the basic knowledge such as tone pattern, part of speech, and antithesis sentences and so on, before they start to appreciate or even compose spring couplets. This workshop will introduce the method of creation and the characteristics of spring couplets with an aim to arise students’ interest in appreciating and writing spring couplets through admiring well-written couplets.

CAC Workshops Review: Handwritten Fai Chun (Red Lucky Scrolls) Workshop

Speaker: Dr. Yim Chi Sing
Date: 7 February 2018/ 14 February 2018
Time: 3:30pm to 5pm / 2:30pm to 4pm
Venue: ELB_403/ YIA_408
Language: Cantonese

Introduction: The red lucky scroll being used during Chinese New Year as traditional decoration is called Fai Chun (揮春) in Cantonese. It is usually in square or rectangular shape, written with lucky phrases, hung on walls or doorways for good luck and prosperity. Nowadays, Fai Chun is mostly in printed versions, but owe to its variety in content and value in calligraphy, handwritten Fai Chun is still well-received by people. This workshop is for students who are willing to know more about the different forms and planning of content of Fai Chun. The speaker will demonstrate the handwriting skills and participants can also practise and write proper and elegant Fai Chun for themselves and their loved ones.
